Overuse of Overlays Research

April 23, 2026

The following information was obtained from an article by the Nielsen Norman Group titled “Overuse of Overlays.” Lightboxes have been studied on their effect on usability. Although overlays are beneficial to provide context, they could be detrimental if they are not properly used. It is important to use the “Five W’s” to determine whether an overlay is appropriate.

The observations concerning mobile accessibility were quite enlightening to me. Overlays that hide certain content or do not offer an easy way out could prove catastrophic to accomplishing tasks. This means that, when designing, I need to include "close" instructions that are clearly visible and that are also reachable using the Escape button. To learn more, the approach used by Apple from using overlays to dedicated pages is exemplary.

Form Design Best Practices

April 14, 2026

“Best Practices For Form Design” is highlighted that the main aim of any form on the web should be to achieve conversions. The most interesting idea discussed in the article is related to the principle of lowering the level of cognitive burden on users. This could be achieved through limiting the number of fields and employing one-column designs.

Additionally, I have understood the significance of "affordance" using the technique of field sizing. Making the width of the field the same size as the amount of data to be entered acts as a visual indicator for the user. The article clearly illustrates that making web pages accessible should not be considered an add-on; instead, it is a basic responsibility.
